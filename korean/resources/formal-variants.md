# Formal/Literary Variants of Basic Korean

Common TOPIK 2 words that are formal or emphatic versions of TOPIK 1 vocabulary.

## Adverbs

### 오직 vs 만
- **만** (particle): 나만 알아 - Only I know
- **오직** (adverb): 오직 너만 사랑해 - I love only you
- 오직 is emphatic, literary
- Found in: songs, poetry, dramatic statements

### 마치 vs 처럼
- **처럼** alone: 꿈처럼 - like a dream
- **마치 + 처럼**: 마치 꿈처럼 - just like a dream (emphatic)
- 마치 adds literary/descriptive emphasis
- Found in: storytelling, descriptive writing

### 너무나 vs 너무
- **너무**: 너무 좋아 - I like it so much
- **너무나**: 너무나 아름다워 - So very beautiful
- 너무나 is emotional/literary emphasis
- Low added value for learners

### 정말로 vs 정말
- **정말**: 정말? - Really?
- **정말로**: 정말로 그랬어 - It really happened
- Slightly more emphatic, but minimal difference
- Low added value for learners

## Connectives

### 또한 vs 도/그리고
- **도** (particle): 나도 갈래 - I want to go too
- **그리고**: 그리고 뭐? - And what?
- **또한**: 또한 이 점도 고려해야 합니다 - This point must also be considered
- 또한 is formal written Korean
- Found in: essays, news, presentations

### 게다가 vs 그리고
- **그리고**: neutral "and"
- **게다가**: "furthermore" / "on top of that"
- Adds nuance of escalation or addition
- Found in: both formal and semi-casual

### 따라서 vs 그래서
- **그래서**: 그래서 뭐? - So what?
- **따라서**: 따라서 결론은... - Therefore, the conclusion is...
- 따라서 is academic/formal "therefore"
- Found in: essays, news, formal speech

### 즉
- Means "in other words" / "namely"
- No simple casual alternative
- Used to rephrase or clarify a point
- Found in: explanations, definitions

## For TOPIK Test

Essential for:
- Reading comprehension (news, essays)
- Writing section (formal register expected)

Words like 따라서, 또한, 게다가, 즉 are frequently tested.

## For Casual Learning

- **Recognize**: 오직, 마치, 또한 - you'll encounter in media
- **Learn**: 게다가, 즉 - occasionally useful in conversation
- **Skip**: 너무나, 정말로 - adds little value
